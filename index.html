So What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is an infectious disease that had it's first case in December of 2019.
It became a worldwide pandemic at around mid-January as case numbers started to increase.
It reached America in January of 2020. At first, it was just a mild case and nobody really
thought that it was any serious, until the disease spread rapidly and there were numerous
death cases in the US. Then, the government took action. We are going to show the cases and
deaths caused by COVID-19 overtime in the US alongside trends
observed over the top 10 states with the most deaths and cases.

    Our Results

    After graphing our data, we determined that in the US, Total Deaths peaked in December.
    We believe this is because of the travel season and that maybe, like the flu and common cold,
    COVID-19 spreads easier in the cold. We then saw rapid decline of deaths coming into June, however
    We also saw continued increases in COVID-19 cases. We believe the decreased trends of deaths and cases
    is due to increased vaccination levels. We also saw an increased risk for getting infected or dying from
    covid-19 in Women over 85 years compared to men. However, we also saw an increased risk in Men from
    75-84 compared to women.
